Why South West London?

Unlike St Neots, a market town with strong employer networks but lower visibility of support services, South West London presents a different challenge - a dense urban environment with a complex employment landscape.

Key factors supporting the launch of a Citizen Hub in this area:

  • Employment & Skills Mismatch – Many businesses struggle to recruit locally, while jobseekers face barriers such as low confidence, digital exclusion, and lack of employer connections.
  • High Potential for Employer Engagement – SW London is home to thriving industries, corporate HQs, and entrepreneurial networks, providing strong partnership opportunities for business-led training and recruitment.
  • Accessibility Barriers to Jobcentres – The nearest Jobcentre Plus is not easily accessible for many residents, making a local, community-based alternative essential.
  • Local Anchor Institutions – Key partners offer a unique opportunity to engage businesses, young people, and the wider community in employment and skills initiatives.

About Us

The Citizen Hub is a concept that was born in St Neots, Cambridgeshire, and has been a resounding success, now entering a new and exciting phase of roll out across the country.

We are looking to create a Citizen Hub SW London as part of the Citizen Hub network, with a separate P & L, separate governance, its own local context, but accessing all the good bits and the learnings from the first hub.

Our Impact Goals

Launching in 2025, we’re targeting measurable outcomes to empower South West London:

  • Volunteer Hours Logged: 5,000+
  • Jobs Placed: 40 in Year 1, scaling to 300+ by 2026
  • Training Programme Completions: 300+ participants
  • Corporate Partnerships Secured: 50 businesses

Our journey begins with prep in February 2025, ramps up in May, and fully launches in September—aiming for 1,000+ participants by 2026.

Rollout Plan

Our phased launch builds momentum for Citizen Hub SW London:

  • Feb 2025: Prep – Research needs, develop branding, and secure early stakeholder support.
  • Mar 2025: Investor Event – Attract funders with mission and impact goals, targeting £135,000 by May.
  • May 2025: Soft Launch – Start workshops and job fairs, recruit volunteers, and build partnerships.
  • Jul-Aug 2025: Staffing – Hire key team members and finalize operations.
  • Sep 2025: Full Launch – Host a launch event, share success stories, and expand outreach.

Ongoing engagement and evaluation will drive growth into 2026.
